Odin's Borscht

Borscht is a vibrant and hearty Eastern European beet soup renowned for its deep red color and rich, earthy flavors. Typically served with a dollop of sour cream and fresh dill, it combines grated beets, potatoes, and a medley of vegetables, creating a satisfying and traditional dish enjoyed both hot and cold.
Ingredients:
  • 4 medium-sized beets, peeled and grated
  • 2 large potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 2 carrots, peeled and grated
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 4 cups vegetable broth
  • 2 cups water
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 teaspoon dried dill (or 2-3 sprigs of fresh dill)
  • 1 tablespoon white vinegar (or more to taste)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Sour cream or yogurt (for garnish, optional)
  • Fresh dill or parsley (for garnish, optional)
Instructions:
  1. Prepare the Vegetables:
    • Heat the vegetable oil in a large soup pot over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and minced garlic. Sauté for a few minutes until the onion becomes translucent.
    • Add the grated beets, carrots, and diced potatoes to the pot. Sauté for about 10 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the vegetables start to soften and the beets release their color.
  2. Add Liquids and Seasonings:
    • Pour in the vegetable broth and water. Add the bay leaves and dried dill (if using fresh dill, reserve it for garnish).
    • Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to a simmer. Cover and cook for about 20-25 minutes or until the vegetables are tender.
  3. Season and Finish:
    • Stir in the white vinegar, salt, and pepper. Start with a tablespoon of vinegar and adjust to taste. The vinegar gives borscht its characteristic tang.
    • Remove the bay leaves.
  4. Serve:
    • Ladle the hot borscht into bowls. You can serve it as is or top each bowl with a dollop of sour cream or yogurt and a sprinkle of fresh dill or parsley for added flavor and garnish.
  5. Optional Step for Cold Borscht:
    • If you want to serve cold borscht, let it cool to room temperature and then refrigerate it for several hours or overnight before serving. Cold borscht is a refreshing summer dish.
Enjoy your homemade borscht! You can adjust the ingredients and seasonings to suit your taste preferences, and it's often served with crusty bread or a slice of rye bread.
