Odin's Meatball

Meatballs are savory, bite-sized spheres of ground meat, often mixed with herbs, spices, and breadcrumbs for added flavor and texture. They are a versatile culinary delight, commonly served in various cuisines and enjoyed in dishes such as spaghetti and meatballs or as appetizers.
Ingredients:
For the Meatballs:
  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 1/2 pound ground pork (or use more ground beef if you prefer)
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 1/2 cup breadcrumbs
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 1 egg
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground allspice
  • 1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • Butter or oil for frying
For the Gravy:
  • 2 cups beef or vegetable broth
  • 1/2 cup heavy cream
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • Salt and pepper to taste
Instructions:
  1. Prepare the Meatballs
    • In a small bowl, combine the breadcrumbs and milk. Let them sit for a few minutes until the breadcrumbs have absorbed the milk.
    • In a large mixing bowl, combine the ground beef, ground pork, finely chopped onion, soaked breadcrumbs, egg, salt, pepper, allspice, and nutmeg. Mix everything thoroughly until well combined.
  2. Shape the Meatballs:
    • With wet hands to prevent sticking, roll the mixture into small meatballs, about 1 to 1.5 inches in diameter. You should get approximately 30-40 meatballs from this mixture.
  3. Fry the Meatballs:
    • In the same skillet, add a bit more butter or oil if needed.
    • Sprinkle the flour into the skillet and whisk it into the remaining fat to create a roux. Cook for a minute or two until it turns lightly golden.
    • Gradually whisk in the beef or vegetable broth and heavy cream, ensuring there are no lumps.
    • Simmer the gravy, stirring constantly, until it thickens, which should take about 5-7 minutes.
    • Season the gravy with salt and pepper to taste.
  4. Combine and Serve:
    • Add the cooked meatballs back to the skillet with the gravy.
    • Simmer for a few more minutes until the meatballs are heated through and the flavors meld.
  5. Serve:
    • Serve the Swedish meatballs hot with lingonberry sauce on the side, and optionally, over a bed of egg noodles or mashed potatoes.
Enjoy your homemade Swedish Meatballs!
